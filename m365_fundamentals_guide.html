Identity & Sign-In — Entra ID (Azure AD)
The foundation of all M365 services — Authentication, MFA, SSO, Conditional Access
1
What is Entra ID and why does it exist?

Every time you sign in to Microsoft 365 — whether it's Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, or OneDrive — you are NOT signing in to each app separately. You sign in to one central place called Entra ID (previously called Azure Active Directory). Entra ID is Microsoft's cloud-based identity service. Think of it as the "master key system" for your entire organization.

🏢 Real World Analogy
Imagine a large office building with 50 rooms (50 apps). Instead of giving each employee a different key for every room, the building has ONE smart security badge system. The employee scans their badge once at the entrance → the building verifies their identity → that single verified identity gives access to every room they're allowed in. Entra ID is that smart badge system for Microsoft 365.
✅ Real Example
Priya signs in to portal.office.com with her company email and password. After she's verified, she can open Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, Power BI — all without signing in again. That seamless access is called Single Sign-On (SSO), powered by Entra ID.
2

3
What is MFA and Why is it Critical?

MFA stands for Multi-Factor Authentication. A password alone can be stolen through phishing or data breaches. MFA adds a second verification — something only YOU physically have.

🏧 Real World Analogy
An ATM needs TWO things: your card (something you have) AND your PIN (something you know). If someone steals your PIN but doesn't have your card, they get nothing. MFA applies the same principle to your Microsoft account. Even if a hacker steals your password, they cannot sign in without your phone.

The three MFA factors are: Something you know (password, PIN), Something you have (phone, hardware token, smart card), Something you are (fingerprint, face recognition).

✅ Real Example
Rahul's password gets phished. The attacker tries to sign in from Russia. Entra ID sends an MFA push to Rahul's phone in Pune. The attacker can't approve it — they don't have the phone. Entra blocks the sign-in. Rahul gets a notification about the suspicious attempt. Account is safe. MFA stopped the breach.
4
Conditional Access — Smart Rules for Sign-In

Conditional Access (CA) lets admins set rules that control when and how users can sign in. Instead of a simple password check, CA evaluates multiple signals before granting access.

🚦 Real World Analogy
Think of CA like an intelligent traffic light system at a border crossing. The system checks: Is this vehicle registered? Is the driver licensed? Is there a travel ban? Is it a suspicious time? Based on all these signals, it decides: let through freely, ask for inspection, or block completely. CA evaluates: Who is signing in + From where + On what device + To which app + At what risk level → then decides the action.
CA Signal: User/Group
Is this user in the Finance team? Guest? Admin?
CA Signal: Location
Is sign-in from trusted IP or corporate office?
CA Signal: Device
Is device enrolled in Intune and compliant?
CA Action: Grant
Allow access, require MFA, or require compliant device
CA Action: Block
Deny access entirely based on risk signals
Report-Only Mode
Test policy without enforcing — see who would be affected
✅ Real Example
Company policy: "All users accessing SharePoint from outside the office must use MFA." In office (trusted IP) → sign in with just password. From home or coffee shop → MFA required. From an unmanaged personal PC → blocked entirely. One CA policy handles all three scenarios automatically.
5

Email — Exchange Online
How email works end-to-end — DNS, SMTP, mailboxes, connectors, security records
1
What is Exchange Online?

Exchange Online is Microsoft's cloud-based email service. It replaced the traditional on-premises Exchange Server — instead of running email servers in your data center, Microsoft runs them for you in the cloud. Every M365 license that includes email provisions an Exchange Online mailbox — typically 50GB or 100GB of storage per user.

📮 Real World Analogy
Traditional email servers are like owning your own post office building — you buy the building, hire staff, maintain sorting machines, and pay for everything yourself. Exchange Online is like using the national postal service — you pay a fee per employee, and Microsoft runs all the infrastructure. You just focus on sending and receiving mail.
2
How an Email Travels — The Complete Journey

When you click "Send" in Outlook, your email goes through a specific technical journey. Understanding this journey helps you diagnose why emails fail to deliver.

The MX record (Mail eXchange record) in DNS tells the internet: "To deliver email to @contoso.com, send it to THIS server." This is why updating MX records is the critical step during email migrations — it redirects where the world sends your mail.

✅ Real Example
Ananya at TCS (tcs.com) emails Rohan at Infosys (infosys.com). TCS's mail server looks up the MX record for infosys.com in DNS. DNS responds: "send to infosys-com.mail.protection.outlook.com" (Infosys uses EXO). TCS server connects to that address and delivers the email. Rohan sees it in Outlook seconds later.
3
The Three Critical DNS Records — SPF, DKIM, DMARC

These three DNS records work together to fight email spoofing and spam. Without them, your emails land in junk folders or get rejected. Every support engineer must know what these do.

📦 Real World Analogy
Imagine someone sends you a package claiming to be from Amazon. How do you verify it's real?

SPF = Checking if the delivery truck is on Amazon's approved carrier list.
DKIM = The package has a tamper-proof seal only Amazon can create — proves it wasn't opened or faked in transit.
DMARC = Amazon's official policy: "If our SPF or DKIM check fails, here's what to do with the package — deliver it, quarantine it, or return to sender (reject)."
RecordFull NameWhat It DoesExample Value
SPFSender Policy FrameworkLists which servers are allowed to send email for your domainv=spf1 include:spf.protection.outlook.com -all
DKIMDomainKeys Identified MailAdds a cryptographic signature to every outbound email — proves it wasn't tampered withTwo CNAME records added in Exchange Admin Center
DMARCDomain-based Message AuthenticationTells receiving servers what to do when SPF or DKIM fails (none/quarantine/reject)v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:dmarc@contoso.com
💡 Always start DMARC with p=none (monitoring only). Monitor for 2–4 weeks, fix legitimate sending sources, then graduate to p=quarantine, then p=reject. Jumping straight to p=reject breaks legitimate email flows.
4

✅ Real Example
A company has: 500 user mailboxes (one per employee), 10 shared mailboxes (support@, hr@, info@), 30 distribution lists (department email groups), 15 room mailboxes (conference rooms), and 25 M365 Groups (project teams with Teams + SharePoint). All managed from the Exchange Admin Center at admin.exchange.microsoft.com.
5
Mail Flow — Connectors and Transport Rules

Connectors are configured paths for email. An inbound connector tells EXO how to receive email from specific sources (like a third-party security gateway). An outbound connector tells EXO where to route outgoing email (like through an on-premises server in hybrid setup).

Transport Rules (also called Mail Flow Rules) are if-this-then-that rules that apply to all email in transit. Examples: "If email is sent to external recipients, add a disclaimer." "If email contains 'confidential', apply encryption." "If sender is in HR, allow all external sharing." These rules apply before email lands in any mailbox.

✅ Real Example
A bank creates a transport rule: "If outbound email contains a credit card number pattern (16 digits), block delivery and notify the compliance team." This is implemented in Exchange Online as a transport rule using a sensitive information type regex — it runs on EVERY outgoing email automatically, protecting against accidental data leaks.
6

Many large organizations are in a hybrid state — some mailboxes are still on on-premises Exchange servers, and some are already in Exchange Online (the cloud). This is normal during a migration that takes months or years.

Microsoft Teams
Chat, Meetings, Calling, Channels — and what actually powers it all behind the scenes
1
What is Microsoft Teams? The Big Picture

Teams is Microsoft's collaboration hub — it combines chat, video meetings, voice calling, file sharing, and apps into one interface. But here's what most beginners don't know: Teams is not a standalone product. It is an interface built on top of other M365 services. Understanding what powers Teams underneath helps you troubleshoot it effectively.

🏨 Real World Analogy
Think of Teams like a hotel. You see the lobby, the rooms, the restaurants, the gym. But behind the scenes, there's a plumbing system (SharePoint), an electrical system (Exchange Online), a phone system (Microsoft Calling), and a security system (Entra ID). If any of those backend systems breaks, the hotel experience breaks even though the hotel building itself looks fine. This is why Teams issues often have to be fixed in SharePoint, Exchange, or Entra — not in Teams itself.
2

✅ Real Troubleshooting Example
The Files tab in a Teams channel is blank for all members. This is NOT a Teams bug. The Files tab is a SharePoint document library. The SharePoint admin accidentally changed the permissions on the backing site, breaking inheritance. Fix: Go to SharePoint → restore permissions → Files tab comes back automatically. You never had to touch Teams at all.

Teams Meetings — How Audio and Video Work

Teams meetings use WebRTC technology over UDP ports to transmit audio and video. UDP is preferred because it's fast — a few dropped packets cause brief glitches, which is better than TCP's guaranteed delivery causing pauses and freezes in real-time media.

Corporate firewalls often block UDP traffic (they prefer TCP which is easier to inspect). When UDP is blocked, Teams falls back to TCP relay — this works but with much higher latency. This is the #1 cause of "Teams audio is choppy at the office but works perfectly at home." The home router allows UDP; the corporate firewall doesn't.

5
Teams Calling — PSTN and Direct Routing

Teams can replace your phone system entirely. PSTN (Public Switched Telephone Network) is the traditional telephone network — the one that reaches any phone number in the world. Teams connects to PSTN in two ways:

6
Teams Policies — How Admins Control Features

Admins control what features users can access through Teams policies. There are separate policies for meetings, messaging, calling, app access, and more. The key insight about policies:

🎯 Policy Priority Explained
Imagine a game with three sets of rules: Personal rules (given to you specifically), Group rules (given to your club), and Worldwide rules (the default everyone follows). Your personal rules ALWAYS win. If you don't have personal rules, your group rules apply. If no group rules, worldwide rules apply. In Teams: Direct assignment > Group assignment > Global (org-wide default). This is why "I applied the policy to the group but the user still has the old behavior" — the user has a direct assignment overriding the group policy.

SharePoint Online
Sites, Libraries, Permissions, Search — the file storage backbone of Microsoft 365
1
What is SharePoint Online?

SharePoint Online is Microsoft's web-based file management and collaboration platform. It provides websites (sites) where teams store files, create pages, manage lists, and collaborate. It's also the behind-the-scenes storage for Teams channels, OneDrive, and M365 Groups.

🏗️ Real World Analogy
Think of SharePoint as a building complex. Each building is a "Site" (like a department's building). Inside each building are floors (Libraries) where files are organized. Each floor has rooms (Folders). The building has a security desk (Permissions) that decides who can enter which floor. There's also a building directory (Search) that can find any document across all buildings instantly.
2
SharePoint Structure — Sites, Libraries, Lists, Pages
Site Collection
The top-level container. Each department or project gets a site. URL: contoso.sharepoint.com/sites/HR
Document Library
A folder system within a site for storing files. Has versioning, metadata columns, and check-out. Like a smart filing cabinet.
List
A table of data in SharePoint. Like Excel but collaborative. Used for tasks, contacts, issues, approvals. Each row = one item.
Page
A web page you design and publish in SharePoint — like an intranet homepage, a department news feed, or a company wiki.
Hub Site
A parent site that connects multiple department sites together — provides unified navigation and search across all associated sites.
App Catalog
Where custom solutions and third-party apps are deployed to SharePoint sites by administrators.
✅ Real Example
Infosys has a Hub Site for all internal sites. Connected to it: HR Site (with Policies Library, Training Videos Library, Job Openings List), Finance Site (Reports Library, Budget Tracker List), IT Site (Procedures Library, Incidents List). All have unified search and navigation through the hub. An employee searching "travel policy" from the hub instantly finds the document in the HR site — even without going there directly.
3

4
SharePoint Versioning — Never Lose a File

Every time someone saves a document in SharePoint, a version is created. You can restore any previous version at any time. This is what makes SharePoint-stored files resilient against accidental overwrites and ransomware.

✅ Real Example
A finance analyst accidentally deletes 3,000 characters of data from a financial model and saves it. She realizes the mistake 2 days later. In SharePoint: Click file → Version history → Find the version from 2 days ago → Restore. The file is back exactly as it was, in 10 seconds, with zero data loss. Without versioning (like a network file share), this data would be permanently gone.
⚠️ Versioning has a hidden cost: every version stores the full file. A 10MB Excel file with 100 versions uses 1GB of SharePoint storage. Set version limits (50 major versions is usually sufficient) to prevent storage quota issues.
5

OneDrive for Business
Personal cloud storage — sync, sharing, versioning, KFM — explained simply
1
What is OneDrive for Business?

OneDrive for Business is personal cloud storage for each employee. When your company assigns you an M365 license, you automatically get a OneDrive — typically 1TB of personal storage. It lives at contoso-my.sharepoint.com/personal/yourname_contoso_com (it's actually built on SharePoint technology underneath).

🗄️ Real World Analogy
OneDrive is like a personal desk drawer at work. It's yours — you decide what goes in it and who (if anyone) can see specific items. Compare to SharePoint, which is like the shared filing room for the whole department — everyone with access can see everything stored there.

Practical rule: Put YOUR personal work files in OneDrive. Put files the TEAM needs to access in SharePoint (or Teams channel Files).
2
How OneDrive Sync Works

The OneDrive sync client is a program that runs in the Windows/Mac system tray. It creates a folder on your local computer that mirrors what's in your cloud OneDrive. When you save a file to the OneDrive folder, it automatically uploads to the cloud. When someone shares a file with you, it syncs down.

When he gets back to his desk, his laptop already has the updated version. Zero manual file transfer. Zero email attachments. All automated by the sync client.
3

4
Known Folder Move (KFM) — Backing Up Desktop and Documents

Known Folder Move (KFM) is a feature that automatically redirects a user's Desktop, Documents, and Pictures folders to sync with OneDrive. This means all work files saved to the Desktop are automatically backed up — even if the laptop dies, all data is safe in the cloud.

🌧️ Real World Analogy
Without KFM, when an employee's laptop is lost or stolen, everything on their Desktop and Documents is gone forever. With KFM enabled, it's like having a real-time automatic backup running silently in the background. The employee gets a new laptop, signs in to OneDrive, and their Desktop looks exactly the same as before. Zero data loss.
💡 IT deploys KFM via Intune or Group Policy. Users often don't even notice KFM running — they just see the OneDrive icon in their Desktop/Documents folder properties. This is one of the highest-value, lowest-cost IT wins available in M365.

Microsoft Intune — Device Management
MDM vs MAM, Autopilot, Compliance Policies — how IT manages devices without touching them
1
What is Microsoft Intune?

Intune is Microsoft's cloud-based device management solution. It allows IT administrators to manage laptops, phones, and tablets — configuring settings, pushing apps, enforcing security policies, and wiping devices remotely — all without physically touching the device. It's part of the Microsoft Endpoint Manager suite.

🎮 Real World Analogy
Without Intune, IT is like a driving instructor who has to get in every single car manually to check mirrors, adjust settings, and verify the driver has a license. With Intune, IT is like a fleet management control center — you see all vehicles on one dashboard, push configuration changes to all of them simultaneously, get alerts when any vehicle breaks a rule, and can remotely disable a vehicle if it's stolen. All from one screen, without leaving the office.
2
MDM vs MAM — The Two Ways Intune Works

Intune manages devices in two fundamentally different ways, depending on whether IT owns the device or the employee does:

MDM — Mobile Device ManagementMAM — Mobile App Management
Who owns device?Company (corporate-owned)Employee (personal/BYOD)
What does IT manage?The ENTIRE device — OS settings, WiFi, VPN, certificates, all appsOnly the work APPS (Outlook, Teams, Edge) — not the personal device
ExampleIT can see device encryption status, push Office, enforce screen lockIT can force a PIN to open Outlook, prevent copy-paste from Outlook to personal apps, wipe ONLY corporate data
Best forCompany laptops, company-issued iPhonesBYOD — employees using personal phones for work email
✅ Real Example
A company has 500 corporate laptops (MDM-enrolled — IT manages everything) and 300 employees using personal iPhones for Outlook (MAM-only — IT only manages the Outlook app). An employee's personal iPhone is lost: IT uses Intune to selective wipe — removes only the corporate Outlook data and removes the device's access to company email. The employee's personal photos, WhatsApp, and other apps are completely untouched.
3

4
Shared Computer Activation — For VDI and RDS Environments

In a normal setup, one license = up to 5 devices. But in a VDI (Virtual Desktop Infrastructure) or RDS (Remote Desktop Services) environment, 100 users might log in to the SAME server at different times. You can't assign 100 licenses to 1 machine. The solution is Shared Computer Activation (SCA).

With SCA enabled, activation is per-user-session instead of per-machine. Each user who logs in to the VDI/RDS gets their own temporary 12-hour activation token tied to their personal license. When they log out, the token is released. The machine has no persistent activation — users bring their own license at login time.

2
DLP — Data Loss Prevention

DLP policies automatically detect and protect sensitive information as it moves through M365 — email, Teams chats, SharePoint files, OneDrive files. DLP uses sensitive information types (pre-built regex patterns for credit cards, SSNs, passport numbers, etc.) to identify sensitive content and then takes action.

🔍 Real World Analogy
DLP is like an X-ray machine at airport security. Every bag (email/file) going through gets scanned automatically. The machine is pre-programmed to recognize dangerous items (sensitive data patterns). If it finds something suspicious, it alerts the security officer (compliance alert), holds the bag (blocks email), or stamps it as "reviewed" (policy tip notification). This happens to ALL bags — the person carrying it doesn't have to do anything differently.
✅ Real Example
A bank employee accidentally emails a spreadsheet with 500 customer credit card numbers to an external Gmail address. Without DLP: Email delivers. Compliance breach. Potential fine. With DLP: DLP policy detects 500+ credit card number patterns in the attachment. It blocks the email from delivering, shows the user a policy tip ("This email may contain sensitive information — are you sure?"), and alerts the compliance team with full details. Data breach prevented automatically, in milliseconds.
3
Sensitivity Labels — Classify and Protect Content

Sensitivity labels are tags you apply to documents and emails to classify their sensitivity level. Unlike DLP which acts on content patterns, sensitivity labels can encrypt files, restrict who can open them, add watermarks, and prevent forwarding — based on the classification applied.

Public
Anyone can see this. No restrictions. Annual report, public website content.
Internal
Employees only. Can be shared internally but not externally.
Confidential
Specific teams only. Adds encryption. Only assigned users can open the file.
Highly Confidential
Maximum protection. Encrypted. Cannot be forwarded. Watermarked. Audit logged.
✅ Real Example
An HR manager applies the "Highly Confidential — HR Only" label to a salary spreadsheet in SharePoint. What happens automatically: The file is encrypted. Only HR department members can open it (verified at the time of opening, not just at download). If anyone forwards the file to an external person, the external person cannot open it — they get an "Access Denied" message because encryption checked their identity against the allowed list. The watermark "HIGHLY CONFIDENTIAL" appears on every page when printed.
4
Audit Logs — The "Security Camera" of M365

The Microsoft Purview Audit Log records virtually every user and admin action in M365 — who signed in, who downloaded what file, who deleted an email, who changed a permission, who created a new inbox rule. It's the forensic trail for security investigations and compliance audits.

✅ Real Scenario
An employee is suspected of stealing customer data before resigning. The compliance team searches the Audit Log for that user: Week before resignation: Audit shows 2,000 file downloads from SharePoint, mass email to personal Gmail (caught by DLP — but DLP logs are also in audit), creation of an inbox forwarding rule to personal email. This audit trail becomes evidence in the HR investigation. Without audit logs, there would be no way to prove what happened.
⚠️ Audit logging is NOT enabled by default in older tenants. Always verify it's on: Purview Compliance Portal → Audit → Search. If you see "Start recording activity," it was never turned on. Enable it immediately — you cannot recover past events once they're not logged.
5
Microsoft Defender for Office 365 — Email Threat Protection

Defender for Office 365 protects against malicious emails, phishing, and malware. It adds threat intelligence on top of Exchange Online's basic spam filtering.

Safe Attachments
Opens every email attachment in a virtual sandbox (isolated environment) before delivering it. If the attachment does something malicious in the sandbox, it's blocked. Zero-day protection.
Safe Links
Rewrites every URL in emails. When user clicks a link, Safe Links checks the URL in real-time at click-time (not just at delivery). Catches links that became malicious after delivery.
Anti-Phishing
AI-powered analysis detecting impersonation attempts, spoofed domains, and suspicious sender behavior.
